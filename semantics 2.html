Facts about cats

Key Features of Cats: Scientific Name: Felis catus Classification: Domesticated mammal, part of the Felidae family Subfamilies: Pantherinae (roaring) and Felinae (purring) Characteristics: Retractable claws, powerful bodies, acute senses, long tails, specialized teeth for hunting. Purring: Likely caused by a special pad in the vocal cords that vibrates at low frequencies. Domestication: Believed to have originated in the Near East around 7500 BC. .

Breeds and Lifespan: There are over 60 recognized cat breeds. Siamese, British Shorthair, and Maine Coon are some of the well-known breeds. Cats typically live between 13 and 20 years. An average adult cat weighs between 4 and 5 kilograms. Additional Facts: Cats have 230 bones, while humans have 206. Cats have a special organ called the Jacobson's organ that allows them to taste scents. Cats' whiskers are mood indicators. Cats love catnip, especially the real catnip (Nepeta Cataria). Cats mark their owners as possessions.
